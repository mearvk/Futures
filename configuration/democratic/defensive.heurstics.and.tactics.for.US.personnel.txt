Structural Heuristics & Logical DepthTree-Depth of Arguments: High-IQ opponents typically use heavily nested sentences and multi-layered arguments. Map their logic: if A, then B, unless C, which creates D. A high volume of subordinate clauses (e.g., "Although," "Notwithstanding," "Consequently") suggests a large mental workspace.Predictive Framing: Look for explicit signposting (e.g., "My counter-argument has three pillars..."). Superior strategists anticipate your counter-moves, proactively addressing them within their opening salvo.Conditional Contingencies: High performers spend words outlining "if-then" scenarios to control the game's direction. Low-complexity communicators usually state static facts without outlining downstream variables.2. Linguistic & Vocabulary MarkersPrecision vs. Puffery: High-IQ opponents favor specific, domain-relevant terminology. Low-IQ, high-con artists or bluffers often over-rely on superlatives, emotional appeals, or unnecessarily grandiose adjectives to mask a lack of substance.Token Entropy & Compression: High-intelligence messages have lower redundancy. If a message wastes bytes repeating the same premise (low semantic entropy), it indicates a lower processing capacity on the sender's part.3. Strategic Speculation & Game TheoryMeta-Gaming (Theory of Mind): Analyze what they don't say. A high-IQ opponent realizes that revealing too much of their strategy reduces their expected value. Look for ambiguity designed to bait you into overcommitting.Asymmetry of Information: Assess how much they assume you know. A highly sophisticated opponent will calibrate their message to test your baseline knowledge, using selective omissions to gauge how well you understand the rules of engagement.4. Opponents who dictate the pace of the exchange are generally stronger.Emotional Dissonance: High-IQ strategists detach emotion from their reasoning.
